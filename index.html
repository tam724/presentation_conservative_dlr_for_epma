Variational Formulation and (Semi-)Discretization

Dynamical Low Rank Approximation

rank-adaptive basis-update galerkin (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$)
K-Step Integrate $\mathbf{K}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{V}_0] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{V}_0] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{V}_0, \quad \mathbf{K}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0. \end{equation}
L-Step Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t, \quad \mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T. \end{equation}
Basis Augmentation Compute the matrices $\mathbf{\hat{U}}$ and $\mathbf{\hat{V}}$ whose columns form an orthonormal basis of the range of $(\mathbf{K}(t_1), \mathbf{U}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$ and $(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times \tilde{r}}$. S-Step Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{\hat{r}\times \tilde{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ governed by the matrix differential equation \begin{equation}\label{eq:s_step} [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}, \quad \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_0) = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T\mathbf{U}_0]\, \mathbf{S}_0 \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T\mathbf{\hat{V}}]. \end{equation} Truncation Compute the truncated SVD $\mathbf{P}_1\mathbf{S}_1\mathbf{Q}_1^T \approx \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_1)$ and set $\mathbf{U}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{U}}\mathbf{P}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r_{\text{new}}}$ and $\mathbf{V}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{V}}\mathbf{Q}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r_{\text{new}}}$. The new rank $r_{\text{new}}$ can be chosen adaptively by truncating the singular values $\sigma_i$ of $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_1)$ such that the norm of the trailing singular values $||(\sigma_{r_\text{new} + 1}, ..., \sigma_{\min(\hat{r}, \tilde{r})})||_2 < \vartheta$ is below a given tolerance $\vartheta$ or, alternatively, for a fixed rank $r_{\text{new}}=r$. \cite{https://doi.org/10.1137/23M1565103} additionally proposes a step-rejection if $r_\text{new}=2r$ followed by restarting the algorithm with the already augmented bases $\mathbf{\hat{U}}$ and $\mathbf{\hat{V}}$ instead of $\mathbf{U}_0$ and $\mathbf{V}_0$ leading to a potential rank increase to $4r$. \end{description} The rank $r_{\text{new}}$ approximation of the solution $\mathbf{Y}$ at $t_1$ is then given by $\mathbf{Y}(t_1) \approx \mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{S}_1 \mathbf{V}_1^T$.
