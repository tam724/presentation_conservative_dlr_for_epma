Adjoint Linear Transport for Computational Modeling in EPMA

  • spatial domain $x \in \mathcal{R} \subset \mathbb{R}^3$, direction $\Omega \in S^2$, energy $\epsilon \in [\epsilon_0, \epsilon_1] \subset \mathcal{R}^+$
  • Notation: $\langle \cdot \rangle = \int_{\mathcal{R}} \int_{S^2} \cdot \textrm{d}\Omega \textrm{d}x$, $[\cdot]_{\partial_x^\pm} = \int_{\partial \mathcal{R}} \int_{n \cdot \Omega \gtrless 0 } \cdot \textrm{d}\Omega \textrm{d}x$
Primal System
\begin{align} -\partial_\epsilon(s \psi) + \Omega \cdot{} \nabla_x \psi + \Sigma^{\text{tot}} \psi & = \int_{S^2} \Sigma \psi' \textrm{d} \Omega'\\ \text{ with } \psi|_{\partial \mathcal{R}, n \cdot \Omega < 0} = g^{\text{bc}} & \text{ and } \psi|_{\epsilon_1} = 0 \\ \mathcal{Y} = \int_{\epsilon_0}^{\epsilon_1} \langle h^{\text{vol}} \psi \rangle & \textrm{d} \epsilon \end{align}
  • valuable to investigate/understand physics
Adjoint System
\begin{align} s\partial_\epsilon(\phi) - \Omega \cdot{} \nabla_x \phi + \Sigma^{\text{tot}} \phi & = \int_{S^2} \Sigma \phi' \textrm{d} \Omega' + h^{\text{vol}}\\ \text{ with } \phi|_{\partial \mathcal{R}, n \cdot \Omega > 0} = 0 & \text{ and } \phi|_{\epsilon_0} = 0 \\ \mathcal{Y} = -\int_{\epsilon_0}^{\epsilon_1} &[n \cdot \Omega g^{\text{bc}} \phi]_{\partial_x^-} \textrm{d} \epsilon \end{align}
  • very valuable to compute complete measurement data sets
Properties of the primal system:
  • Local dissipation of a weighted L2-energy (entropy) $\mu(x, \epsilon) = s(\epsilon, x) \int_{S^2} \psi^2(\epsilon, x, \Omega) \textrm{d}\Omega$.
  • Local conservation of a mass density $\rho(\epsilon, x) = s(\epsilon, x) \int_{S^2} \psi(\epsilon, x, \Omega) \textrm{d}\Omega$.
  • Local dissipation/conservation lead to global dissipation/conservation (up to boundary fluxes and sources).


Implications for a discretization:
  • Ability to compute the adjoint system(s) is crucial.
  • A discretization of the adjoint should not yield different results (dual-consistency).

Dynamical Low Rank Approximation

rank-adaptive basis-update galerkin (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
Starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$)
K-Step Integrate $\mathbf{K}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{V}_0] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{V}_0] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{V}_0, \quad \mathbf{K}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0. \end{equation}
L-Step Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t, \quad \mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T. \end{equation}
Basis Augmentation Compute $\mathbf{\hat{U}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{K}(t_1), \mathbf{U}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$ and $\mathbf{\hat{V}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times \tilde{r}}$.
S-Step Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{\hat{r}\times \tilde{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$ \begin{equation}\label{eq:s_step} [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}, \quad \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_0) = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T\mathbf{U}_0]\, \mathbf{S}_0 \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T\mathbf{\hat{V}}]. \end{equation} Truncation Truncate the SVD $\mathbf{P}_1\mathbf{S}_1\mathbf{Q}_1^T \approx \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_1)$ and set $\mathbf{U}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{U}}\mathbf{P}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r_{\text{new}}}$ and $\mathbf{V}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{V}}\mathbf{Q}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r_{\text{new}}}$.
