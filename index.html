Adjoint Linear Transport for Computational Modeling in EPMA

Linear Transport Model for EPMA
  • stationary linear transport in continuous slowing down approximation

    $-\partial_\epsilon(s \psi) + \Omega \cdot{} \nabla_x \psi + \Sigma^{\text{tot}} \psi = \int_{S^2} \Sigma \psi' \textrm{d} \Omega'$

  • experimental setup: boundary conditions

    $\psi|_{\partial \mathcal{R}, n \cdot \Omega < 0} = g^{\text{bc}} \text{ and } \psi|_{\epsilon_1} = 0$

  • observations/measurements: linear functionals $$\mathcal{Y} = \int_{\epsilon_0}^{\epsilon_1} \int_{\mathcal{R}} h^{\text{vol}} \int_{S^2} \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega \textrm{d} x \textrm{d} \epsilon$$
$\to$ valuable to investigate/understand physics
Primal System
$$\mathcal{L} \psi = g; \, \mathcal{Y} = \langle h, \psi \rangle$$
  • with many beams setups $g$, little observation extractions $h$
  • solve instead:
Adjoint System
$$ \mathcal{L}^* \phi = h; \, \mathcal{Y} = \langle \phi, g \rangle $$
  • very valuable to compute complete measurement data sets
  • does (almost) not scale in the number of beam setups $g$
Implications for a Discretization:
  • Discrete primal and adjoint systems should yield the same results (dual-consistency).
  • The $s$-weighted scalar flux $\rho = s\int_{S^2} \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega$ must be well approximated.

    local conservation law: $\quad -\partial_\epsilon \rho + \nabla_x \cdot (\int_{S^2} \Omega \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega) = 0$

    • stopping power $s$ = energy loss per distance travelled
    • zeroth moment influences measurement
  • Local dissipation of a $s$-weighted L2-energy $\mu = s\int_{S^2} \psi^2 \, \textrm{d} \Omega$.

Mixed-Variational Formulation and (Semi-)Discretization

  • split the (phase-space) flux $\psi$ into even/odd part $\psi^\pm$

    $\psi^\pm(-\Omega) = \pm \psi^\pm(\Omega)$

  • use a mixed variational formulation:
    couple even/odd spherical harmonics $\Upsilon^\pm(\Omega)$ with $L^2/H^1$ spatial bases

    $\psi(x, \epsilon, \Omega) \approx \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^+(x)^T \Psi^+(\epsilon) \Upsilon^+(\Omega)}_{\approx \, \psi^+(x, \epsilon, \Omega)} + \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^-(x)^T \Psi^-(\epsilon)\Upsilon^-(\Omega)}_{\approx \, \psi^-(x, \epsilon, \Omega)}$

$\to$ leads to a coupled kronecker-structured matrix-semidiscretization
\begin{alignat}{4} & [\mathbf{N}^+_e] (-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \Psi^+)) [\mathbf{I}^+] & & - [\mathbf{\nabla}_d] \Psi^- [\mathbf{\Omega}_d] & & + [\mathbf{N}^+_e] (\tau_e \Psi^+) [\mathbf{I}^+ - \mathbf{k}^+_e] + [\mathbf{\partial}_d] \Psi^+ [\mathbf{|\Omega|}_d] & & + \mathbf{b}^+ = 0 \\ & [\mathbf{N}^-_e] (-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \Psi^-)) [\mathbf{I}^-] & & + [\mathbf{\nabla}_d^T] \Psi^+ [\mathbf{\Omega}_d^T] & & + [\mathbf{N}^-_e] (\tau_e \Psi^-) [\mathbf{I}^- - \mathbf{k}^-_e] & & + \mathbf{b}^- = 0. \end{alignat}
short notation:

$[\mathbf{M}] \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{b}$

Scalar Flux Conservation: $-\partial_\epsilon \rho + \nabla_x \cdot (\int_{S^2} \Omega \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega) = 0$
(scalar) flux $\rho$ in $V_h$
boundary flux over $\partial V_h$
with $\mathbf{W}$, $\mathbf{W}^T\mathbf{W} = I$. For conservation we must exactly fulfill

\begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{N} \mathbf{W}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{W}] + \mathbf{b} \mathbf{W} \end{equation}

Energy integration: implicit trapezoidal rule (dual: implicit midpoint at half-energy steps)

Dynamical Low-Rank Approximation

rank-adaptive basis-update galerkin (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode:
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
Starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$):
K-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{K}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{K}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{V}_0] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{V}_0] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{V}_0. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{U}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{K}(t_1), \mathbf{U}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$.
L-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{V}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times \tilde{r}}$.
S-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{\hat{r}\times \tilde{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_0) = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T\mathbf{U}_0]\, \mathbf{S}_0 \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T\mathbf{\hat{V}}]$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}. \end{equation} Truncate: $\mathbf{P}_1\mathbf{S}_1\mathbf{Q}_1^T \approx \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_1)$ and set $\mathbf{U}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{U}}\mathbf{P}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r_{\text{new}}}$ and $\mathbf{V}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{V}}\mathbf{Q}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r_{\text{new}}}$.

If $\mathbf{V}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{W} = \mathbf{W}$ (if the columns of $\mathbf{W}$ are $\in \text{span}(\mathbf{V}_0)$), the K-step satisfies local conservation.

$\to$ use $\mathbf{K}(t_1)$ to enforce conservation. (Only updates $\mathbf{\hat{U}}$.)

"Find truncation strategy such that $\mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{S}_1 \mathbf{V}_1^T \mathbf{W} = \mathbf{K}(t_1) \mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{W}$ holds exactly"

Conservative Low-Rank Integration

a mass-conservative variant of rank-adaptive (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode:
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
Starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$):
L-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{V}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0, \mathbf{W}) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$.
Augmented K-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0[\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{\hat{V}}]$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}. \end{equation}
Truncation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{W}} = \mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{W}$ and the QR decomposition $\mathbf{\tilde{V}} \mathbf{\tilde{R}} = \mathbf{\hat{V}} - \mathbf{W}\mathbf{\hat{W}}^T$ (rank-revealing).
Truncate the SVD of the $\mathbf{W}$-orthogonal part $\mathbf{\tilde{P}}_1 \mathbf{\tilde{S}}_1 \mathbf{\tilde{Q}}_1^T \approx \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_1)\mathbf{\tilde{R}}^T$. Compute the left basis as the QR decomposition $\mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{R} = \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_1) \mathbf{\hat{W}}, \mathbf{\tilde{P}}_1$ \begin{equation} \mathbf{Y}(t_1) \approx \mathbf{U}_1 \underbrace{\mathbf{R} \begin{pmatrix} I & 0 \\ 0 & \mathbf{\tilde{S}}_1\end{pmatrix}}_{=\mathbf{S}_1} \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{W} & \mathbf{\tilde{V}} \mathbf{\tilde{Q}}_1 \end{pmatrix}^T}_{=\mathbf{V_1}^T} \end{equation}

Shares robustness (in the presence of small singular values) with the BUG integrator

The augmented K-step satisfies the local conservation laws.

The conservative truncation retains the conserved quantities.

The conservative tuncation is stable.
