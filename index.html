Adjoint Linear Transport for Computational Modeling in EPMA

  • spatial domain $x \in \mathcal{R} \subset \mathbb{R}^3$, direction $\Omega \in S^2$, energy $\epsilon \in [\epsilon_0, \epsilon_1] \subset \mathcal{R}^+$
  • Notation: $\langle \cdot \rangle = \int_{\mathcal{R}} \int_{S^2} \cdot \textrm{d}\Omega \textrm{d}x$ and $[\cdot]_{\partial_x^\pm} = \int_{\partial \mathcal{R}} \int_{n \cdot \Omega \gtrless 0 } \cdot \textrm{d}\Omega \textrm{d}x$
Primal System $\mathcal{L} \psi = g; \, \mathcal{Y} = \langle h, \psi \rangle$
\begin{align} -\partial_\epsilon(s \psi) + \Omega \cdot{} \nabla_x \psi + \Sigma^{\text{tot}} \psi & = \int_{S^2} \Sigma \psi' \textrm{d} \Omega'\\ \text{ with } \psi|_{\partial \mathcal{R}, n \cdot \Omega < 0} = g^{\text{bc}} & \text{ and } \psi|_{\epsilon_1} = 0 \\ \mathcal{Y} = \int_{\epsilon_0}^{\epsilon_1} \langle h^{\text{vol}} \psi \rangle & \textrm{d} \epsilon \end{align}
  • valuable to investigate/understand physics
Adjoint System $\mathcal{L}^* \phi = h; \, \mathcal{Y} = \langle \phi, g \rangle$
\begin{align} s\partial_\epsilon(\phi) - \Omega \cdot{} \nabla_x \phi + \Sigma^{\text{tot}} \phi & = \int_{S^2} \Sigma \phi' \textrm{d} \Omega' + h^{\text{vol}}\\ \text{ with } \phi|_{\partial \mathcal{R}, n \cdot \Omega > 0} = 0 & \text{ and } \phi|_{\epsilon_0} = 0 \\ \mathcal{Y} = -\int_{\epsilon_0}^{\epsilon_1} &[n \cdot \Omega g^{\text{bc}} \phi]_{\partial_x^-} \textrm{d} \epsilon \end{align}
  • very valuable to compute complete measurement data sets
Classial Properties of the Primal System:
  • Local dissipation of a weighted L2-energy (entropy) $\mu(x, \epsilon) = s(\epsilon, x) \int_{S^2} \psi^2(\epsilon, x, \Omega) \textrm{d}\Omega$.
  • Local conservation of a weighted (scalar) flux $\rho(\epsilon, x) = s(\epsilon, x) \int_{S^2} \psi(\epsilon, x, \Omega) \textrm{d}\Omega$.
  • Local dissipation/conservation lead to global dissipation/conservation (up to boundary fluxes and sources).


Implications for a Discretization:
  • Ability to compute the adjoint system(s) is crucial.
  • A discretization of the adjoint should not yield different results (dual-consistency).

Mixed-Variational Formulation and (Semi-)Discretization

  • split the (phase-space) flux $\psi$ into even/odd part $\psi^\pm$ \begin{equation} \psi^\pm(-\Omega) = \pm \psi^\pm(\Omega) \end{equation}
  • energy-transient variational formulation (test-function $\phi$) \begin{align} \partial_\epsilon m_\epsilon(\psi(\epsilon), \phi) + a_\epsilon(\psi(\epsilon), \phi) + b_\epsilon(\phi) = 0 \quad \text{ with } \end{align} \begin{align} m_\epsilon(\psi(\epsilon), \phi) &= \langle -s \, \psi\, \phi \rangle\\ a_\epsilon(\psi(\epsilon), \phi) &= \langle \Omega \cdot \nabla_x \psi^+ \phi^- - \phi^- \Omega \cdot \nabla_x \psi^+ + \Sigma^{\text{tot}}\, \psi \, \phi - \int_{S^2} \Sigma \psi' \textrm{d}\Omega' \phi \rangle + [|n\cdot\Omega|\psi^+\phi^+]\\ b_\epsilon(\phi) &= 2[n\cdot\Omega \, g^{\text{bc}}\phi^+]_{\partial_x^-} \end{align}
  • $P_N$ approximation: split spherical harmonics into even/odd $\Upsilon^\pm(\Omega)$, couple with spatial bases ($H^1$/$L^2$) and approximate \begin{equation} \psi(x, \epsilon, \Omega) \approx \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^+(x)^T \Psi^+(\epsilon) \Upsilon^+(\Omega)}_{\approx \, \psi^+(x, \epsilon, \Omega)} + \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^-(x)^T \Psi^-(\epsilon)\Upsilon^-(\Omega)}_{\approx \, \psi^-(x, \epsilon, \Omega)} \end{equation}
  • leads to the kronecker-structured matrix-semidiscretization \begin{alignat}{4} & [\mathbf{N}^+_e] (-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \Psi^+)) [\mathbf{I}^+] & & - [\mathbf{\nabla}_d] \Psi^- [\mathbf{\Omega}_d] & & + [\mathbf{N}^+_e] (\tau_e \Psi^+) [\mathbf{I}^+ - \mathbf{k}^+_e] + [\mathbf{\partial}_d] \Psi^+ [\mathbf{|\Omega|}_d] & & + \mathbf{b}^+ = 0 \\ & [\mathbf{N}^-_e] (-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \Psi^-)) [\mathbf{I}^-] & & + [\mathbf{\nabla}_d^T] \Psi^+ [\mathbf{\Omega}_d^T] & & + [\mathbf{N}^-_e] (\tau_e \Psi^-) [\mathbf{I}^- - \mathbf{k}^-_e] & & + \mathbf{b}^- = 0. \end{alignat}
in summary: $[\mathbf{M}] \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{b}$
  • Egger, H., Schlottbom, M. (2012). A Mixed Variational Framework For The Radiative Transfer Equation. doi:10.1142/s021820251150014x
  • Egger, H., Schlottbom, M. (2014). Numerical methods for parameter identification in stationary radiative transfer. doi:10.1007/s10589-014-9657-9

Conservation Properties

  • weighted (scalar) flux $\rho(\epsilon, x) = s(\epsilon, x) \int_{S^2} \psi \, \textrm{d}\Omega$
  • Local Flux Conservation Law
    \begin{align} -\partial_\epsilon \rho + \nabla_x \cdot J &= 0 \\ n \cdot J &= \int_{n \cdot \Omega > 0} n \cdot \Omega \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega + \int_{n \cdot \Omega < 0} n \cdot \Omega \psi^{\text{bc}} \, \textrm{d} \Omega \end{align}
  • current density $J = \int_{S^2} \Omega \psi \, \textrm{d} \Omega$
  • The flux conservation law is inherited to the matrix-semidiscretization: testing with $\int_{S^2} (\Upsilon^+(\Omega)^T, \Upsilon^+(\Omega)^T)^T \, \textrm{d} \Omega$ yields it's weak form.
    • $\rho$: even moment of $\psi$
    • $J$: odd moment of $\psi$
  • We couple even - $L^2$ and odd - $H^1$. For the flux in a test volume $V_h$, $\mathbf{\mathcal{M}}_{V_h} = \int_{V_h} \rho \,\textrm{d}x$ we have \begin{equation} -\partial_\epsilon \mathbf{\mathcal{M}}_{V_h}(\epsilon) = (\int_{V_h}\mathcal{X}^-(x) \textrm{d} x)^T \cdot ([\mathbf{G}^-])^{-1} \cdot (-[\mathbf{\nabla}_d]^T \cdot \Psi^+ \cdot \int_{S^2} \Omega_d \Upsilon^+ \textrm{d}\Omega) \end{equation}
(scalar) flux in $V_h$
boundary flux over $\partial V_h$
With $\mathbf{W}$, $\mathbf{W}^T\mathbf{W} = I$. For conservation we must exactly fulfill \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{N} \mathbf{W}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \mathbf{Y}(t) [\mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{W}] + \mathbf{b} \mathbf{W} \end{equation}

Dynamical Low Rank Approximation

rank-adaptive basis-update galerkin (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode:
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
Starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$):
K-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{K}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{K}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{V}_0] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{K}(t) \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{V}_0] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{V}_0. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{U}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{K}(t_1), \mathbf{U}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$.
L-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{V}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times \tilde{r}}$.
S-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{\hat{r}\times \tilde{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_0) = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T\mathbf{U}_0]\, \mathbf{S}_0 \, [\mathbf{V}_0^T\mathbf{\hat{V}}]$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{\hat{U}}] \, \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{\hat{U}}^T \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}. \end{equation} Truncate: $\mathbf{P}_1\mathbf{S}_1\mathbf{Q}_1^T \approx \mathbf{\hat{S}}(t_1)$ and set $\mathbf{U}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{U}}\mathbf{P}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times r_{\text{new}}}$ and $\mathbf{V}_1 = \mathbf{\hat{V}}\mathbf{Q}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r_{\text{new}}}$.

If $\mathbf{V}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{W} = \mathbf{W}$ (if the columns of $\mathbf{W}$ are $\in \text{span}(\mathbf{V}_0)$), the K-step satisfies local conservation.

$\to$ use $\mathbf{K}(t_1)$ to enforce conservation. (Only updates $\mathbf{\hat{U}}$.)

"Find truncation strategy such that $\mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{S}_1 \mathbf{V}_1^T \mathbf{W} = \mathbf{K}(t_1) \mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{W}$ holds exactly"

Conservative Low-Rank Integration

a mass-conservative variant of rank-adaptive (BUG) integrator
applied to the linear matrix-ode:
\begin{equation} \mathbf{M} \partial_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{N} = \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{Y}(t) \mathbf{B}_t + \mathbf{b}_t \end{equation}
Starting from $\mathbf{Y}(t_0) \approx \mathbf{U}_0 \mathbf{S}_0 \mathbf{V}^T_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (rank $r$):
L-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{L}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times r}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{L}(t_0)^T = \mathbf{S}_0\mathbf{V}_0^T$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{M} \mathbf{U}_0] \, \partial_t \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{N}] = [\mathbf{U}_0^T \mathbf{A}_t \mathbf{U}_0] \, \mathbf{L}(t)^T \, [\mathbf{B}_t] + \mathbf{U}^T_0\mathbf{b}_t. \end{equation} Augmentation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{V}} = \text{ortho}(\mathbf{L}(t_1), \mathbf{V}_0, \mathbf{W}) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$.
Augmented K-Step: Integrate $\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times \hat{r}}$ from $t_0$ to $t_1$. With $\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_0) = \mathbf{U}_0\mathbf{S}_0[\mathbf{V}_0^T \mathbf{\hat{V}}]$, solve \begin{equation} [\mathbf{M}] \, \partial_t \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{N} \mathbf{\hat{V}}] = [\mathbf{A}_t] \, \mathbf{\hat{K}}(t) \, [\mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{B}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}] + \mathbf{b}_t \mathbf{\hat{V}}. \end{equation}
Conservative Truncation: Compute $\mathbf{\hat{W}} = \mathbf{\hat{V}}^T \mathbf{W}$ and decompose $(\mathbf{\tilde{V}}, \mathbf{\tilde{R}}) = \text{qr}(\mathbf{\hat{V}} - \mathbf{W}\mathbf{\hat{W}}^T)$ (rank-revealing).
Truncate the $\mathbf{W}$-orthogonal part $\mathbf{\tilde{P}}_1 \mathbf{\tilde{S}}_1 \mathbf{\tilde{Q}}_1 = \text{svd}(\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_1)\mathbf{\tilde{R}})$. Compute the left basis as $\mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{R} = \text{qr}(\mathbf{\hat{K}}(t_1) \mathbf{\hat{W}})$ \begin{equation} \mathbf{Y}(t_1) \approx \mathbf{U}_1 \underbrace{\mathbf{R} \begin{pmatrix} I & 0 \\ 0 & \mathbf{\tilde{S}}_1\end{pmatrix}}_{=\mathbf{S}_1} \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{W} & \mathbf{\tilde{V}} \mathbf{\tilde{Q}}_1 \end{pmatrix}^T}_{=\mathbf{V_1}^T} \end{equation}

Shares robustness (in the presence of smalle singular values) with the BUG integrator

The augmented K-step satisfies the local conservation laws.

The conservative truncation retains the conserved quantities.

The conservative tuncation is stable.

Application to the mixed Variational Framework

Combine the low rank approximation with the $2\times2$ (even-odd) block structure of the mixed-variational formulation. We approximate
\begin{equation} \psi(x, \epsilon, \Omega) \approx \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^+(x)^T [\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+_\epsilon} \mathbf{S}^+_\epsilon (\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V}^+_\epsilon})^T] \Upsilon^+(\Omega)}_{\approx \psi^+(x, \epsilon, \Omega)} + \underbrace{\mathcal{X}^-(x)^T [\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-_\epsilon} \mathbf{S}^-_\epsilon (\color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V}^-_\epsilon})^T] \Upsilon^-(\Omega)}_{\approx \psi^-(x, \epsilon, \Omega)} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \begin{alignedat}{4} & [(\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+})^T\mathbf{N}^+_e\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+}] [-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \mathbf{S}^+)] [(\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V}^+})^T\mathbf{I}^+ \color{Blue}{\mathbf{V^+}}] & & - [(\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+})^T \mathbf{\nabla}_d \color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-}] \mathbf{S}^- [(\color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V}^-})^T \mathbf{\Omega}_d \color{Blue}{\mathbf{V^+}}] & & + [(\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+})^T\mathbf{N}^+_e\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+}] [\tau_e \Psi^+] [(\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V}^+})^T (\mathbf{I}^+ - \mathbf{k}^+_e) \color{Blue}{\mathbf{V^+}}] + [(\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+})^T\mathbf{\partial}_d\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+}] \Psi^+ [(\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V}^+})^T\mathbf{|\Omega|}_d \color{Blue}{\mathbf{V^+}}] & & + (\color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+})^T\mathbf{b}^+\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V^+}} = 0 \\ & [(\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-})^T\mathbf{N}^-_e\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-}] [-\partial_\epsilon (s_e \mathbf{S}^-)] [(\color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V}^-})^T\mathbf{I}^- \color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V^-}}] & & + [(\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-})^T \mathbf{\nabla}_d^T \color{Orange}{\mathbf{U}^+}] \mathbf{S}^+ [(\color{Blue}{\mathbf{V}^+})^T \mathbf{\Omega}_d^T \color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V^-}}] & & + [(\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-})^T\mathbf{N}^-_e\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-}] [\tau_e \Psi^-] [(\color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V}^-})^T (\mathbf{I}^- - \mathbf{k}^-_e) \color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V^-}}] & & + (\color{Tomato}{\mathbf{U}^-})^T\mathbf{b}^-\color{MediumPurple}{\mathbf{V^-}} = 0. \end{alignedat} \end{equation}
